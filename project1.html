VEX ROBOTICS
PROGRAM

My earliest engineering work came through a multi-season VEX Robotics program, where I helped design, build, and iterate competition robots from initial concept through elimination rounds.

Each season started from a new game rule set, which meant re-deriving the drivetrain, intake, and scoring mechanism from scratch rather than reusing a fixed design. That cycle — build, test against real opponents, redesign — was my first exposure to the kind of rapid iteration I still rely on in professional CAD work.

Working inside a small team also meant wearing several hats: mechanical design, fabrication, driving strategy, and documentation for judged awards. The team's results across seasons included multiple tournament and design awards.
